Victoria Sponge Cake

What Is Victoria Sponge Cake?

A Victoria sponge cake (a.k.a. a Victoria sandwich) is a British dessert that consists of jam and cream sandwiched between two delicate cake layers. The dish gets its name from Queen Victoria, who is said to have made a habit of enjoying the cake with her afternoon tea.

Cake Ingredients

  • Baking spray
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour (such as Gold Medal)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter or margarine, softened
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ½ cup whole milk, room temperature
  • ½ cup strawberry or raspberry preserves

  • Vanilla Whipped Cream

    • ¾ cup heavy whipping cream
    • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar, plus more for dusting
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • Fresh strawberries, for garnish (Optional)

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C). Grease two 8-inch round cake pans with baking spray; line bottoms with parchment paper. Lightly spray parchment paper with baking spray.
  2. Sift flour, cornstarch, and baking powder into a medium bowl; whisk in salt and set aside.
  3. Beat sugar and butter with an electric mixer on medium speed until light and fluffy, about 3 to 4 minutes.
  4. Beat in eggs one at a time, then add vanilla. With mixer on medium-low, add flour mixture in 3 batches, alternating with milk. Beat until just combined.
  5. Divide batter between pans and spread evenly. Tap pans on a towel-lined counter to release air bubbles.
  6. Bake for 18–22 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool in pans for 10 minutes, then invert onto wire racks and cool completely.
  7. Remove parchment. Place one cake layer on a plate. Stir preserves until smooth and spread over cake.
  8. Make whipped cream: whisk cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla until stiff peaks form.
  9. Dollop whipped cream over jam and spread evenly.
  10. Top with second cake layer. Dust with powdered sugar and garnish with strawberries (optional).

Test Kitchen Tips

This top-rated user recipe was tested in our test kitchen. Here are some expert tips from recipe tester Tricia Manzanero Stuedeman to help you achieve the best results:

  • Cool cakes parchment side down to prevent sticking and avoid rack markings. This ensures a smooth, attractive top layer.
  • Avoid over-mixing for a tender texture. Beat just until combined, and consider folding in the final dry ingredients by hand for a gentler touch.
  • Tap pans after dividing the batter to eliminate large air bubbles and help spread the batter evenly. Be sure to push batter into corners and edges for a clean finish.
  • Calibrate your oven for accurate temperature. An inexpensive oven thermometer can help prevent under- or over-baking.
